# The Logic of Uncertainty 
## Overview
This chapter explores the logic behind uncertainty and likelihood. Using simulations and probability experiments, I examined how evidence updates belief, how randomness forms patterns, and how predictable behavior can emerge from uncertain data.


## Core Question
How can probability help us reason about uncertainty?

## Data Source
This chapter made use of both generated data and datasets from Python libraries.

## My Projects
* [**01_the_logic_of_evidence.ipynb**](./01_the_logic_of_evidence.ipynb): In this notebook, I explore how probability changes when we condition on new information, and how Bayes’ Theorem formalizes this process of updating beliefs.
* [**02_the_shape_of_uncertainty.ipynb**](./02_the_shape_of_uncertainty.ipynb): In this notebook, I explore how patterns in randomness take shape, and how probability distributions help us make sense of uncertainty.
* [**03_the_certainty_within_uncertainty.ipynb**](./03_the_certainty_within_uncertainty.ipynb): In this notebook, I explore  the magic of the Central Limit Theorem, which shows that the average of many random variables tends to follow a normal distribution, even if the original data is not normally distributed. 

## What I Learnt
* I learnt that probability is not just about randomness, it's also about identifying patterns that emerge from random processes over time.
* I reinforced my understanding of conditional probability and learnt how Bayes’ Theorem can update probabilities when new evidence is introduced.
* I observed that larger sample sizes tend to reveal the underlying structure of a distribution more clearly.
* I learnt that even when data is highly skewed or random, the averages of repeated samples tend to form a normal distribution through the Central Limit Theorem.
* I developed a stronger intuition for how uncertainty can still produce predictable behavior when enough data is observed.
